Definition

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a chronic, progressive neurological disorder that affects movement and other functions. The hallmark of PD is the presence of involuntary shaking (tremor), often accompanied by stiffness, slowness of movement, and loss of balance. These symptoms usually worsen over time. While PD can be difficult to diagnose in its early stages, early detection and intervention can lead to improved quality of life and increased life expectancy for affected individuals.

Causes

The exact cause of Parkinson's disease is unknown, but it is believed to be the result of a combination of genetic, environmental, and cellular factors. The disease typically develops later in life, with the average age of onset being between 60 and 70 years. However, the condition can also affect younger individuals.

Symptoms

  • Shaking or trembling of the body, limbs, or hands
  • Rigidity, or stiffness, in the body and limbs
  • Slowness of movement
  • Impaired balance and coordination
  • Restlessness or the urge to move, known as "restless leg syndrome"
  • Stiffness in the neck and an awkward gait
  • Voice changes
  • Blurred vision and difficulty with depth perception
  • Swallowing problems

Clinical Treatment

  • Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS): In severe cases where medications are not sufficient, DBS can be considered. This involves the placement of electrodes in specific areas of the brain to modulate neural activity and reduce symptoms.
  • Surgical procedures: Depending on the patient's symptoms and condition, various surgical procedures may be recommended. These can include:
    • Thalamotomy: A procedure where a small hole is drilled in the thalamus to alleviate pressure on the dopamine system.
    • Pallidotomy: The destruction of a small area in the putamen to help control motor symptoms.
    • Subthalamotomy: The resectioning of a portion of the thalamus, which helps regulate movement.
  • Physical and occupational therapy: To help maintain muscle strength and improve movement, parkinson patients may require physical and occupational therapy.
